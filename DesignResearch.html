1. Opportunity Framing

Final Opportunity Statement: How might we reduce musculoskeletal strain and communication breakdowns for airport ramp workers during high‑traffic operations by providing real‑time ergonomic feedback and supervisor visibility, without slowing task throughput.

Rationale & Selection: In Part 1, I compared multiple opportunities using SET analysis and a weighted decision screen. Each alternative was evaluated by five weighted criteria to ensure objective prioritization.

Weighted Decision Summary

The Weighted Decision Matrix applied the following priority weights: Impact = 0.30, Feasibility = 0.25, Time to Implement = 0.15, Cost Efficiency = 0.15, and Stakeholder Acceptance = 0.15 (Σ = 1.00). The top opportunity—Robotic and Mechanical Lift‑Assist Systems—was scored as:

  • Impact: 5 × 0.30 = 1.50
  • Feasibility: 4 × 0.25 = 1.00
  • Time to Implement: 3 × 0.15 = 0.45
  • Cost Efficiency: 3 × 0.15 = 0.45
  • Stakeholder Acceptance: 3 × 0.15 = 0.45

Total Score = 3.85 / 5. This ranking confirmed high overall viability and guided the refinement toward the ErgoAssist ramp‑worker ergonomics system as the most practical solution for development. For full calculations and visual matrix, visit https://aryan1359.github.io/hcd-project/#part2.

4. Research Synthesis

Personas (from data)

  • Persona 1 — Luis Santos (Ramp Operator): seeks speed and safety; pain points: awkward lifts, noisy comms, lack of posture feedback.
  • Persona 2 — Amira Lee (Ops Coordinator): needs live visibility; pain points: data lag, inconsistent reporting, cognitive overload.

Empathy Map Highlights

  • Say/Do: improvise tools; rush to meet ETD; use radio/hand‑signals.
  • Think: “Don’t slow me down; make it simple.”
  • Feel: fatigue, frustration, weather stress.
  • Needs: hands‑free UI, large targets, offline‑first design, quick recovery.

5. Major Takeaways (Key Findings)

  1. Adoption hinges on zero‑friction use: tools must be hands‑free or one‑tap to avoid workflow disruption.
  2. Environmental factors dominate ergonomics: gloves, noise, and glare affect posture and comms—solutions must adapt.
  3. Supervisors need real‑time, low‑cognitive dashboards: concise summaries outperform raw data streams.
  4. Trust and transparency drive acceptance: users want clarity on what data is captured and how it benefits them.
