Submission Title: Module 6_User Persona 1_Aryan Yaghobi

Product Opportunity (Context)

Ground‑handling crews at busy airports face fatigue, musculoskeletal strain, and communication challenges during high‑traffic operations. The proposed ErgoAssist System integrates wearable posture sensors, real‑time feedback, AI‑driven workload alerts, and voice/hands‑free interaction to support ramp workers in physically demanding environments. The goal is to improve safety, reduce injuries, and enhance operational efficiency in a human–machine collaborative workflow.

User Persona 1 — “Luis Santos — The Fatigued Ramp Operator”

Luis Santos

The Fatigued Ramp Operator

Relation to Opportunity: Core user for ErgoAssist; daily tasks are physically intensive and time‑critical, directly aligned with ergonomic and communication features.

Background: Luis is a 34‑year‑old ramp agent at a major international airport. He has eight years of experience and works rotating 10‑hour shifts in all weather conditions, managing luggage loading, aircraft pushback, and coordination with flight crews.

Goals (Main Objectives)

  • Complete each shift safely and on schedule without injury.
  • Maintain physical stamina and avoid chronic strain (back, shoulders).
  • Communicate clearly and complete tasks despite noise and time pressure.

Behaviors (Actions / Habits)

  • Frequently lifts heavy loads (50+ lb) and bends under aircraft cargo doors.
  • Works long shifts with minimal rest; improvises tool usage when needed.
  • Uses hand signals and radio to coordinate amid high noise and bustle.
  • Checks smartphone for work alerts but often faces weak/offline connectivity.

Motivations (Decision Drivers)

  • Pride in teamwork and reliability for crew and airline.
  • Prefers tools that help him work smarter, not harder.
  • Values recognition for safety compliance and reduced down‑time.

Pain Points (Challenges / Frustrations)

  • Repetitive strain injuries (shoulders/back) from awkward postures and lifting.
  • Communication breakdowns in noisy ramp environment, especially at night.
  • Lack of real‑time feedback on unsafe posture or fatigue until pain is acute.
  • Equipment failures or connectivity issues that delay tasks and increase load.
