Empathy Maps — ErgoAssist

Purpose: capture key insights from observations and interviews for two core personas.

Persona 1 — Luis Santos (Ramp Operator)

SAYS

  • “Don’t slow me down—make it simple.”
  • “Gate change? Tell me early.”
  • “These gloves make small grips tough.”

DOES

  • Rushes to meet ETD; improvises tools for tight angles.
  • Switches hand-signals/radio in high noise.
  • Repositions often in cramped spaces.

THINKS

  • Hands-free or one-tap is essential.
  • Won’t trust tech that fails in rain/glare.
  • Wants fewer re-handles/strains as proof.

FEELS

  • Fatigued from repetitive awkward lifts.
  • Frustrated by glare/rain/noise.
  • Proud when turn-times improve.

Persona 2 — Amira Lee (Ops Coordinator)

SAYS

  • “I need real-time status, not end-of-shift reports.”
  • “One screen I can read at a glance.”
  • “Don’t flood me—only priority alerts.”

DOES

  • Monitors multiple gates; triages during push windows.
  • Checks logs; briefs leads; escalates as needed.
  • Balances on-time performance with safety.

THINKS

  • Needs explainable indicators (no black box).
  • If not actionable fast, it’s noise.
  • Trends over time > raw streams.

FEELS

  • Overloaded by cluttered dashboards.
  • Confident with clear, actionable alerts.
  • Protective of crew trust/privacy.
