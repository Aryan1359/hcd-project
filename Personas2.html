Submission Title: Module 6_User Persona 2_Aryan Yaghobi

Product Opportunity (Context)

Ground‑handling crews at busy airports face fatigue, musculoskeletal strain, and communication challenges during high‑traffic operations. The proposed ErgoAssist System integrates wearable posture sensors, real‑time feedback, AI‑driven workload alerts, and voice/hands‑free interaction to support ramp workers in physically demanding environments. The goal is to improve safety, reduce injuries, and enhance operational efficiency in a human–machine collaborative workflow.

User Persona 2 — "Marcus Lee — The Ramp Operations Coordinator"

Relation to Opportunity: Managerial user of ErgoAssist dashboards; coordinates multiple ramp crews, schedules tasks, and oversees safety performance in real time.

Background : Marcus is a 41‑year‑old shift supervisor responsible for coordinating ground‑handling crews across three concourses. He oversees safety compliance, task scheduling, and ramp operations efficiency for 60+ daily flights.

Goals (Main Objectives)

  • Maintain real‑time awareness of crew performance and task progress.
  • Ensure safety and timing benchmarks are met with minimal incidents.
  • Reduce injuries and absenteeism through proactive monitoring and coaching.
  • Keep flight departures on schedule across multiple gates.

Behaviors (Actions / Habits)

  • Monitors live task dashboards from office and mobile device.
  • Cross‑checks safety reports and plans break rotations for crews.
  • Communicates with dispatch and ramp leads via radio/voice channels.
  • Uses spreadsheets/manual logs to rebalance workload during peaks.

Motivations (Decision Drivers)

  • Committed to crew well‑being and a strong safety culture.
  • Wants predictive tools that flag risks early with clear explanations.
  • Values concise visual summaries over scattered manual reports.
  • Seeks smoother coordination with HR/ops for smarter shift planning.

Pain Points (Challenges / Frustrations)

  • Limited real‑time visibility into individual fatigue or posture quality.
  • High cognitive load managing many crews and incident reports simultaneously.
  • Manual documentation delays and inconsistent reporting from the field.
  • Pressure to reduce incidents while maintaining throughput and on‑time performance.
