Module 5 – Practice: User Interviews (Ramp Worker Ergonomics)

Student: Aryan Yaghobi Course: Human-Centered Design Methods I Context: Integrated Final Assessment – Part 1

Interview Plan (Quick)

Objective: Hear ramp workers’ and supervisors’ needs, pain points, and adoption barriers for ergonomic aids that do not slow operations.
Participants: (1) Ramp Agent (frontline) • (2) Ramp Supervisor/Safety Lead
Method: 1:1 semi-structured interviews • 15–25 minutes each • Notes + optional audio (with consent)
Logistics: Location: break room / quiet corner • Timing: off-peak or post-shift • Tools: phone timer, notes app, phone voice recorder

Consent & Ethics

  • Explain purpose (class project), voluntary participation, no personal identifiers, no company secrets. Ask: “Is it okay if I take notes? Audio recording optional.”
  • Safety first: do not interview on the ramp during active operations.

Script (10 prompts)

  1. Can you walk me through a typical turn when loading/unloading bags?
  2. Where does the work feel most physically demanding?
  3. What postures or motions bother you the most during a busy day?
  4. How do weather and time pressure change the way you work?
  5. What tools or aids (if any) help reduce strain? What makes you use or avoid them?
  6. Tell me about a recent moment when you felt discomfort or risk. What happened?
  7. If you could change one thing about the workflow or space, what would it be?
  8. How would you feel about a wearable or lift-assist that claims to save effort but adds steps?
  9. What would make a new tool easy to adopt for your team (training, speed, trust)?
  10. Anything else I should know to make this job safer without slowing you down?

Interview #1 — Ramp Agent (Frontline)

Type: 1:1 semi-structured • Duration: ~18 min • Setting: break room after afternoon turn
ParticipantRamp Agent, 6 years
ContextHot day (~95°F), tight 30-min turn, narrow-body cargo hold
Top Needs
  • Reduce bending/twisting in tight holds
  • Keep pace—no extra steps that slow the team
  • Better grip and comfort in extreme weather
Pain Points
  • Awkward crouch inside hold; hitting knees/shoulders
  • Lift-assist tools feel bulky; setup takes time
  • Heat makes gloves slippery and drains energy
Quotes
  • “If it adds even 30 seconds per cart, we won’t use it.”
  • “I know the right posture, but when the clock is ticking, you just move.”
  • “Give me something low-profile that doesn’t get in the way.”
Ideas/Workarounds
  • Rotate roles every 15–20 min to avoid overuse
  • Use knee pads/headlamp; quick stretch during belt jams
  • Preference for lighter, wearable support vs. big devices
Opportunities
  • Low-profile support for crouching/stacking
  • Weather-adaptive gloves with better grip
  • Micro-coaching cues that are private and not disruptive

Interview #2 — Ramp Supervisor / Safety Lead

Type: 1:1 semi-structured • Duration: ~22 min • Setting: quiet corner near ops office
ParticipantRamp Supervisor, 12 years (Safety lead)
ContextCool morning, mixed crew (new + experienced), two simultaneous turns
Top Needs
  • Reduce injury rates without hurting on-time performance
  • Simple training; quick to learn for new hires
  • Clear ROI evidence for any new tool
Pain Points
  • Low adoption of assist devices after the first week
  • New hires copy bad habits under time pressure
  • Hard to justify gear if ROI isn’t obvious
Quotes
  • “Anything that slows a turn will collect dust.” — If a new tool slows baggage handling, crews will abandon it.
  • “We need proof—less strains, fewer call-outs.” — Supervisors want measurable reductions in injuries and absences.
  • “Make it fool-proof and it’ll stick past training day.” — Simple, intuitive designs sustain adoption beyond day-one training.
Ideas/Workarounds
  • Micro-training: 2-minute tips at start of shift
  • Visual cues in the hold for better stacking posture
  • Trial gear on one gate; measure incidents vs. baseline
Opportunities
  • Lightweight assist that doesn’t require setup
  • Adoption metrics dashboard (incidents, pace, use rate)
  • Starter kit for new hires (knee pads, gloves, quick guide)
